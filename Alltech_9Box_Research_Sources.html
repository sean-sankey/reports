Sources: The 9-Box Rollout Traps

Research cited in the companion brief covering what typically goes wrong with 9-box implementations.

  • Academic
    DeNisi & Murphy: "Performance Appraisal and Performance Management: 100 Years of Progress?"
    Journal of Applied Psychology, 2017. 30-year review concluding calibration processes amplify rater biases rather than neutralising them.
  • Academic
    Deloitte: "Reinventing Performance Management"
    Harvard Business Review, 2015. Landmark paper disclosing Deloitte abandoned ratings after finding systems measured rater bias more reliably than employee performance.
  • Academic
    Tomas Chamorro-Premuzic: "The Talent Delusion"
    UCL/Columbia research showing correlation between managers' subjective potential ratings and actual career performance sits around 0.3, barely above chance.
  • Industry
    CEB/Gartner: Talent Management Research
    Found 72% of employees remain in the same 9-box position for two or more years regardless of actual performance change. Also found tiered talent systems reduce discretionary effort in the bottom 80%.
  • Industry
    McKinsey: Women in the Workplace (2022)
    Found women rated lower on potential than men with equivalent performance scores in 4 out of 5 sectors studied.
  • Industry
    Deloitte: Global Human Capital Trends (2019)
    Found organisations using 9-box systems allocate 3x more development spend per capita on high-potentials than on core performers, despite core performers representing 70-80% of workforce.
  • Industry
    RHR International: Succession Readiness Research
    10-year longitudinal study of 500 senior roles. Only 55% of "ready now" succession candidates performed at or above expectations when promoted.
  • Industry
    Gallup: "State of the American Manager"
    Attributes 70% of variance in employee engagement scores to quality of direct line management.
  • Media
    Vanity Fair: "Microsoft's Lost Decade"
    Kurt Eichenwald's investigation into Microsoft's stack ranking system, calling it "the most destructive process in all of Microsoft."
  • Practitioner
    Marcus Buckingham: "Nine Lies About Work"
    ADP Research Institute. Argues talent review systems surface the best-networked and most visible people, not the most talented.
  • Practitioner
    Peter Cappelli, Wharton School
    Research on high-potential identification failure: organisations confuse confidence with competence, visibility with value, and similarity with potential.

Sources: The Case For the 9-Box

Research cited in the companion brief covering why the 9-box works when implemented with discipline.

  • Industry
    McKinsey & Company: "The War for Talent" Research
    Companies using structured talent review frameworks reported 20% increase in employee engagement. Companies with regular talent review are 2.4x more likely to have "ready now" successors for critical roles.
  • Industry
    McKinsey 9-Box Talent Matrix: Case Study
    Documents a multinational company that reduced workforce turnover by 25% through active succession planning culture built around the 9-box.
  • Practitioner
    Talent Strategy Group: "It's Not About the Boxes"
    Marc Effron (former VP Talent Management, Avon). The grid is a forcing function for quality talent conversations, not a classification system.
  • Practitioner
    AIHR: "9-Box Grid: A Practitioner's Guide"
    Research finding the 9-box had a positive influence on employee performance, with the effect growing significantly stronger when directly linked to career development activities.
  • Practitioner
    Quantum Workplace: "A Smarter Approach to Evaluating Performance & Potential"
    Evidence for the 9-box as a tool to move from gut-instinct talent decisions to structured, consistent assessment.
  • Industry
    Gallup: Employee Engagement and Continuous Feedback
    Companies engaging in continuous feedback have 14.9% lower turnover rates.
  • Practitioner
    Josh Bersin: HR Industry Analysis
    Argues companies that abandoned the 9-box failed because they used it to judge people instead of develop them: a culture flaw, not a model flaw.
  • Case
    Unilever: Talent & Organisation Strategy
    Unilever's promote-from-within culture, filling 80%+ of senior leadership roles from internal talent. 9-box adopted in early 2000s as pipeline infrastructure.
  • Case
    PepsiCo: Leadership Pipeline
    40+ year evolution of 9-box approach. Quarterly review rhythm, multi-source input, continuous iteration to challenge assessment biases.
  • Case
    Siemens: Leadership Development
    Global 9-box deployment across 300,000+ employees. Grid used as common visual language across geographies, with emphasis on development plans over placement.
  • Industry
    Culture Amp: "Pros and Cons of Using a 9-Box Grid for Succession Planning"
    Balanced practitioner analysis of when the 9-box adds value for succession and when it doesn't.
  • Practitioner
    HR Curator: "Humpty Dumpty and the 9-Box"
    Five steps to rebuilding the 9-box using the science of leadership potential. Practical implementation guidance.
