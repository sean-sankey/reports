Alltech Dublin: How to Plant Seeds
Without Over-Pitching

CJ's commercial intelligence brief for Sean ahead of the 16 April workshop. Six opportunity threads, two stakeholders, and a clear view on what to say, what to hold, and what to sequence over the summer.

Prepared by: CJ (Commercial Intelligence) For: Sean Sankey Date: 12 April 2026 Workshop: Thursday 16 April 2026, Alltech, Dublin

Commercial Posture for Thursday

Thursday is not a pitch. It is a diagnostic and thinking session where Sean earns the right to go deeper. The goal is to leave Orla and Robbie with two things: something genuinely useful they didn't have when they walked in, and a clear sense that Sean is someone they want to think with again. Both of those outcomes are worth more than any proposal Sean could table in the room.

The seeds below are not conversation starters. They are background intelligence. Most of them should not be mentioned at all on Thursday. What matters is that Sean has them in his peripheral vision, so he can recognise an opening when it arrives, and respond from capability rather than scrambling to construct something on the spot.

Rule 1Listen for what keeps them up at night. That is where the next engagement lives.
Rule 2Only surface a seed if they open the door. Don't force it. Don't list your services.
Rule 3Be curious about their world, not promotional about yours. Curiosity is the best commercial tool.
Rule 4Leave space for the follow-up. The session doesn't close a deal. It earns the next conversation.

  • His coaching interest is the fastest path to a billable relationship. Don't oversell it. Just acknowledge it.

The Six Seeds

Prioritised by: how naturally they emerge from Thursday's conversation, how ready Sean is to respond to them, and how commercially significant they are. Priority 1 seeds can be acknowledged in the room if the door opens. Priority 2 seeds are mid-term follow-ups. Priority 3 seeds are summer or later.

1Now
Thinking Partner / Relationship Builder
Thinking Partner to Orla on the 9-Box Rollout
The easiest continuation to earn. Low friction, high value, entirely natural given Thursday's context.

Orla is heading to the US over summer. The rollout continues in her absence. She needs someone she trusts who understands what she is trying to do and can help the internal team stay on track, problem-solve as issues emerge, and maintain fidelity to the original intent. That is a natural role for Sean to offer, and it barely feels commercial because it is simply the extension of Thursday's relationship.

This is not a formal engagement pitch. It is a logical continuation. The offer, if it surfaces at all, should be something like: "I'm happy to stay in contact as this moves forward - if it's useful to have someone to pressure-test things with, just reach out." That is it. Let her do the work of turning that into a formal arrangement if the fit is right.

Say this
"Happy to be a sounding board as this moves forward. No pressure, just useful to have someone outside the building who knows the context."
Not this
"We could structure a formal thinking partner engagement at X per month..." - too early, too transactional, kills the warmth.
1Now
Executive Coaching / Robbie
Exec Coaching on Emotional and Political Intelligence
Robbie has named this explicitly. It's a live, open door. Acknowledge it; don't force it through.

This is the most commercially immediate opportunity in the room. Robbie has already expressed specific interest in executive coaching around emotional and political intelligence. Sean doesn't need to introduce it. He needs to respond well when Robbie returns to it, which he likely will.

The right posture is to take it seriously, show genuine understanding of what he is dealing with (running two organisations simultaneously, both the group agenda and Keenan's), and make clear that this is work Sean does. Then offer a clear, low-friction next step: a proper conversation after Dublin to explore what it might look like. Don't attempt to scope it in a group workshop setting.

Say this
"The political complexity of running at both group and subsidiary level simultaneously is genuinely distinct territory. I work with leaders in exactly that position. Worth a proper conversation after today."
Not this
"I offer 1-2-1 executive coaching at [rate] per session..." - do not price or package this in the room. Schedule a follow-up call.
2Soon
The Big Prize / Scale Play
The "Other 5000" Programme
The largest opportunity in this account. Don't touch it Thursday. Let the conversation earn it.

Alltech sends 30 people annually through a 4-year Smurfit MBA, a total of 120 at any given time. That is a serious investment in a small cohort. The other 4,880+ employees get nothing equivalent. That gap is the opportunity. Sean could help design and deliver a structured development programme for the broader population, something rigorous and credible but built for scale, not just for the MBA class.

This is a multi-year, multi-touchpoint engagement. It is not a Thursday conversation. The way it surfaces is by listening on Thursday for any frustration Orla or Robbie express about reach, about the gap between their senior investment and the rest of the organisation, or about getting the right behaviours at mid-level. If that frustration is audible, Sean can plant a single sentence: "One of the things I've been thinking about is how organisations bridge the MBA cohort and the wider population. That gap is usually where culture either takes hold or doesn't." Then stop. Let it land. Don't explain Growth Sprints, don't mention price, don't pitch. Just put the idea in the room.

If the door opens
"That's a gap I see in a lot of organisations. The cohort that gets the MBA investment is important, but the real leverage is usually in the 500 people just below them. That's where culture is made or broken."
Not this
Any form of scoping, pricing, or programme description in the room. This needs its own dedicated session. Planting the question is enough.
2Soon
Robbie / Growth / MuchClearer IP
Growth Sprints
Sean's IP, well-priced, directly relevant to a Chief Growth Officer. Hold it back until post-Dublin.

Growth Sprints are Sean's IP riff on Jake Knapp's Design Sprint methodology. They are structured, time-bounded intensive workshops that help leadership teams generate and stress-test growth ideas. Priced at £6,000-10,000 per workshop in previous delivery. This is exactly the kind of commercial-meets-capability thinking that will resonate with Robbie as CGO.

The connection to Jake Knapp is a genuine conversation opener. Knapp built Design Sprints at Google and wrote the book. If Robbie has read it (or heard of it), there's an immediate shared reference point. Sean interviewed Barb Wixom (MIT, enterprise data monetisation) for his podcast, which is itself a signal of the network he operates in. That credibility matters when you are planting the Growth Sprint seed with a commercial leader.

Do not surface this Thursday. Post-Dublin, after the coaching conversation with Robbie is underway, introduce it as something worth exploring. Frame it as: "One of the things I've developed specifically for commercial leaders is a structured way of running growth sessions. Might be worth talking about." Then let him pull.

Post-Dublin framing
"Following on from our conversation, I wanted to mention something I've developed around structured growth sessions for commercial teams. Based on what you're dealing with, it might be relevant."
Not on Thursday
Introducing a second commercial product while still in the opening session. One relationship at a time. Thursday is about 9-box; Robbie's coaching is the first thread to pull.
2Soon
Standard Portfolio / MorePeople Exec
MuchClearer Trainings as MorePeople Exec
Difficult Conversations, Collaboration, Decision Making - available and ready. Wait for a named need before mentioning.

Sean's standard MuchClearer portfolio (Difficult Conversations, Collaboration Tools, Decision Making, Resilience) can be reskinned and delivered under the MorePeople Exec brand. These are proven, priced, and deliverable. The risk is treating them as a product catalogue rather than a response to a named problem. Don't mention them unless Orla or Robbie surface a problem they address.

Most likely to emerge naturally from the 9-box conversation: Difficult Conversations (giving honest feedback about box placement, having the hard succession conversations) and Collaboration (cross-functional alignment when the 9-box triggers competition rather than cooperation). Both of those are organic extensions of Thursday's content.

If they name the problem
"That's something we have a specific programme for - it's one of the most common friction points after a 9-box rollout. Worth talking about separately."
Not this
"We also offer Difficult Conversations, Collaboration, Decision Making, Resilience..." - any list of services in this context is a red flag.
3Later
Joanne Mart / Parallel Track
Presentation Skills Programme (UK)
Joanne is back ~17 April. The proposal has to be excellent. Keep this thread entirely separate from Dublin.

Joanne is Culture and Talent Manager, UK-based, direct report of Orla. She has been on leave and returns around 17 April. The proposal for the UK presentation skills programme (two cohorts, ~12 participants, recommended Option A at around £4,650 + VAT) needs to land in her inbox the week she returns. This is the lower-value but important second foothold in the Alltech account.

Do not mention Joanne or the presentation skills programme at any point on Thursday. Orla and Joanne are in different lanes. If you conflate them, you risk Orla feeling that Thursday was partly a sales visit for another product. It was not. Keep them entirely separate until both are in proper motion. The Joanne proposal is its own stream and needs its own dedicated attention this week, before Dublin if possible.

In Joanne's proposal
Reflect back what Joanne told you, state the solution clearly, price without apology, close with a clear next step. The Debbie and Sam brief on Sean's voice applies directly.
Not this
Any reference to the Dublin visit, Orla's work, or the 9-box in the Joanne proposal. Different relationship, different brief, different positioning.

The Books: How to Use Them

For Orla

The Power of Moments

Chip Heath and Dan Heath

This is a natural fit for a CPO thinking about how to make the 9-box a genuinely meaningful experience for employees rather than just an HR process. The book's central argument - that defining moments shape our sense of engagement far more than day-to-day experience - is directly relevant to how you communicate box placement, development conversations, and succession decisions.

Give it with a brief note that says something like: "This one keeps coming up when I think about how you make these frameworks feel real to people." No need to explain further. Let her make the connection herself.

For Robbie

Data is Everybody's Business

Barbara H. Wixom (MIT)

Barb Wixom is at MIT, researches enterprise data monetisation, and was a guest on Sean's podcast. That is a genuine connection, and it means this isn't just a book gift - it's a signal about the network Sean operates in and the conversations he has. For a CGO at a global business, enterprise data monetisation is exactly the right register.

The gift does a second job: it signals that Sean's thinking extends beyond L&D into commercial and strategic territory. That matters for Robbie's perception of what kind of partner Sean could be over time.

How the Relationship Develops: A Sequence

16 April / In the Room

Earn the relationship

Deliver the workshop brilliantly. Leave Orla and Robbie with something they can use. Acknowledge Robbie's coaching interest warmly but don't scope it. Offer to stay in contact as a sounding board for the rollout. Give the books. Close with warmth and a clear next conversation.

Week of 21 April / Follow-Up

Send a brief debrief note and lock the coaching conversation

A short email reflecting on what landed, acknowledging anything they said they'd take forward, and proposing a specific time to talk with Robbie about the coaching conversation he expressed interest in. Keep it to three short paragraphs. No attachments, no proposals.

Late April / Early May

Robbie coaching conversation + Joanne proposal lands

The coaching scope call with Robbie happens. Separately, Joanne's presentation skills proposal goes in and gets a response. Two threads in motion. Both are billable. Neither is positioned as part of a broader sales campaign - they are just the natural next thing in each relationship.

May / June / Orla's US Summer

Thinking partner to Orla on the rollout

As Orla prepares for the US and the 9-box moves into its next phase, the value of having Sean as an outside sounding board becomes practical, not theoretical. This is where the thinking partner relationship formalises, even informally. Regular check-ins, ad hoc calls, perhaps a day's workshop with the internal team. The summer window is the commercial opportunity.

Summer and Beyond

The wider Alltech picture begins to take shape

With both Orla and Robbie relationships active, the "other 5000" conversation becomes possible. Growth Sprints are on the table for Robbie. The MorePeople Exec training portfolio has a natural entry point through both threads. Alltech becomes a multi-layered, multi-year account. None of that is possible without Thursday going well.

CJ's Commercial Rules for Thursday

One win at a time

The goal Thursday is a great session and a warm relationship, not a scoped pipeline. Leave with one clear next step, not three. Complexity is a signal that you are moving too fast.

Curiosity over capability

Every question Sean asks in the room is worth more than any statement about what he can offer. The best commercial posture is genuine interest in their situation. That is also the truth, which helps.

Robbie's coaching is the fastest path

Of all six seeds, this is the one with the clearest, most direct path to a billable relationship. He has already named the interest. The job is to receive that well and create a space to go deeper. One follow-up call is all it takes.

The "other 5000" is the biggest prize

But it is a summer conversation, not a Thursday one. Plant the idea if the door opens. Let it sit. Come back to it in May once both Orla and Robbie relationships are in motion.

Keep Joanne and Dublin separate

Two parallel footholds is a strength. Conflating them is a risk. Don't mention Joanne in Dublin. Don't mention Dublin in the Joanne proposal. Different relationships, different needs, different timing.

The books do commercial work

The Barb Wixom book signals that Sean's network extends into MIT and enterprise strategy. That is a positioning statement without being a pitch. Use it. The Heath book for Orla is similarly generous and precisely chosen. These are not gifts. They are the beginning of a longer conversation.
