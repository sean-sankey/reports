Key Findings

Four things the flat average hides

  1. Shaun’s Play Maker at 9 is the team’s relational engine. He is the natural source of orchestration, stakeholder facilitation and collaborative glue in and around the room. In a team that runs predominantly on direction and delivery, that energy is load-bearing.
  2. Kelly and Sean share Strategist 8 but run on entirely different fuel. Kelly executes first (I 10, P 3): pace, momentum, get it done. Sean refines first (P 9, I 4): quality instinct, considered pace. They will land in the same place strategically and then experience consistent friction about tempo and standard. That tension is productive when it is named, costly when it is not.
  3. Three of four people at Implementer 7 or above gives this team exceptional delivery capacity. Shaun, Kelly and Jayne default to action. Sean at 4 is the natural counterweight: less urgency to act, more instinct to pause and check. That tension is a feature, not a flaw, as long as it is held intentionally.
  4. This team is excellent at executing known plays and maintaining high standards. Game Changer is Active at best for all four: Shaun and Sean at 6 and 5, Kelly at 3, Jayne at 2. On any challenge that requires genuinely new thinking, the answer is people, process or will to resist the pull toward the familiar.
